Active vs. Passive: The Great Debate

Should your fund manager try to beat the market (Active) or just match it (Passive)? The data tells a clear story.

Trying to Beat the Market is Hard

Because active funds have higher fees, they face a major hurdle. Over time, very few succeed in outperforming their simple, low-cost passive benchmarks.

Data reflects % of U.S. stock funds failing to beat their benchmarks.

The Hidden Drag: How Fees Impact Growth

Fees may seem small, but over decades they can consume a huge portion of your returns. Minimizing costs is the key to long-term success.

Matching a Fund to Your Goal

Different funds are built for different purposes. The main types are based on what they invest in: stocks for growth, and bonds for stability.
